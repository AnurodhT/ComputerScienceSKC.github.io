Computer Science Course

There are various reasons to take it

NCEA

IB

  • Programming:
    Students gain proficiency in programming languages such as Java, Python, or another language, focusing on writing, testing, and debugging code.
  • Algorithms & Data Structures:
    The course covers the design and analysis of algorithms, as well as the implementation and manipulation of data structures, crucial for efficient problem-solving.
  • Computer Systems:
    Students explore the fundamentals of computer architecture, including hardware, software, and networking components, to understand how computers operate at a technical level.
  • Databases:
    The curriculum includes an in-depth study of database systems, covering data storage, retrieval, and management, along with practical applications.
  • Computational Thinking:
    Emphasis is placed on developing skills to decompose complex problems and create effective, logical solutions using computational methods.
  • Ethics & Social Impact:
    The course examines the ethical, legal, and social implications of computing technology, encouraging students to consider the broader effects of their work in computer science.
  • Assessment:
    Students are evaluated through a combination of internal assessments, including a practical software development project, and external written examinations that test theoretical knowledge.
  • Levels:
    The course is offered at both Standard Level (SL) and Higher Level (HL). SL provides a solid foundation in core topics, while HL delves into more advanced content and includes additional assessments.
